About ChartTracker

Ever since I was a child, I have been a big fan of music. In the early ‘80’s, I started listening to Casey Kasem’s American Top 40 as often as I could. As a teenager, I would ride my bike into town just so I could go to the local record store and look at the Billboard Hot 100 Singles chart and handwrite the information into a notebook. For a while I even started creating my own personal Top 40 charts based on my own listening preferences.

Fast-forward a few decades and the music landscape has changed greatly. We’ve gone from records to cassettes, CDs to MP3s, and now the abundance of streaming music options. We’ve gone from over-the-air radio transmissions to satellite radio and internet-based radio stations and streaming services. What hasn’t changed is peoples’ appetite for listening to music spanning the eras of the music recording industry.

The idea for this website is to create a space where music chart data can be found in one central location. I don't want this website to focus on just one particular music chart but to include chart data from many of the various genres of music. Much of the data that is out there now can be viewed but cannot be sorted or customized based on user preferences. The goal is for this website to bridge this current gap at some time in the future.
